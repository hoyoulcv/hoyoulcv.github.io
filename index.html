Research Interests

My research interest lies in the decisions of agents. What choices should be 'do the right thing' when an agent make every time given certain conditions or environments? Furthermore, what is the process through which the agent makes these choices? How can these decisions be evaluated? Are there specific criteria for evaluation? If there are multiple agents, how should they be handled? I have many questions and curiosities. Some of these curiosities might already have been researched and answered. However, if I pursue a doctoral degree, I aspire to conduct research on these curiosities.

After graduating from Chungnam National university, I worked as a system engineer at Samsung Electronics, writing device drivers for smartphones. However, I decided to pursue further studies at UNM. When I entered UNM, I was a student who loved mathematics and had a strong interest in system programming. I aspired to be like Steve Wozniak and was passionate about subjects like networks and Geometric & probability. However, after taking courses such as Intro to A.I. and Logical Reasoning, I realized there was a whole new world awaiting exploration. My understanding of mathematics underwent a significant change. I discovered that the deductive system in mathematics ultimately can be based on first-order logic and that a substantial portion of it is grounded in philosophy. This realization led me to delve into books like Aristotle's Organon, Boole's Laws of Thought, and Frege Reader. I studied these books extensively, driven not by grades but by a genuine thirst for knowledge. Moreover, observing Professor Jed Crandall(now in ASU), who taught the network course, briefly made me dream of becoming a professor like him. However, the U.S. subprime mortgage crisis had a severe impact on the Korean economy, and I faced financial difficulties as well. Consequently, I contemplated returning to Korea to work at LG IOT lab.
